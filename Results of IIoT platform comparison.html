As mentioned earlier, HCL did extensive comparison of IIoT w.r.t suitability to Lilly's needs. Detailed analysis of results as follows:

  • Generic public platforms have clear edge over verticalized platforms especially from reliability and scalability perspective. Although verticalized platforms have inherent advantage of industry/domain knowledge, however generic platform vendors either are building the domain capability in house or are leveraging partnerships. E.g. Azure and Thingworx partnership. Various solutions deployed on these generic platforms (especially in the smart manufacturing space) are a leading indicator of the same.
  • As per HCL's analysis, among public platforms Azure and AWS clear leaders and are neck to neck in most of the parameters (detailed scoring in the following sections), however Azure has slight edge (especially seen from IIoT context) as they have formed a formidable combination with Thingworx where each side will bring complementary strengths. However, considering Lilly's specific requirements and operating context (for example, preference of Lilly's enterprise team for AWS, limited usage/need for AR, need for high reliability etc.), HCL's suggestion would be to consider AWS at par with Azure. This view also gets reinforced by looking at the overall score for both the platforms (i.e. 0.19 for Azure and 0.17 for AWS) indicating that both these platforms are reasonably positioned for serving Lilly's current and future needs.
  • Lilly can leverage a combination of Thingworx and Azure. This will enable Lilly to have "best of both worlds" as Azure is strong in cloud and Thingworx is strong on edge. This would also be in alignment with Lilly enterprise team's strategy of following "hybrid multi-cloud".
  • In verticalized platform category, PTC's Thingworx is a clear winner with appreciable gap with its close competitor i.e. Mindsphere (Thingworx overall score is 0.14 vis-a-vis 0.08 for Mindsphere).
  • Open Source platforms (Eclipse being the leader in this category) are still in infancy and can't be considered as a serious competitor (overall score is 0.06) to the licensed platforms.
