Energy monitoring is a 3-phase development work. Phase-I, which is a Descriptive & Diagnostics analytics phase, is complete, with HCL as partner. The HVAC Air handling units in multiple sites LCC and LTC were chosen for the management view of energy consumption.

Phase 1 was to visualize energy consumption trends collectively for all AHUs or a single AHU for a particular building or all buildings on one site or all sites with-in a date range. This monitoring will help to:

  • Spot the AHU/building/site where more energy is consumed for any particular day or days, which can be further investigated manually.
  • To investigate, the parameters that affects the AHU having relatively more energy consumption.
  • Active faults visual insight for AHU with-in a date range to identify the AHU with maximum fault count for further investigation.
  • Comparing Active faults between two or more AHUs will help to investigate why one AHU has more faults than others do.

Instrumentation for this phase-I was not done in terms of energy monitoring but only for manufacturing. No specific new devices/sensors were used for monitoring AHU's energy management data, but it was all process parameters used from OSI PI database.

Therefore, in short, local PI storage was required for the reason of-

  • Local analytics requirements.
  • Need to run operations in manufacturing.

Comments/ Observations -

  • Naming convention of tag values in PI system, at all sites differ.
  • In future, there could be a possibility, that apart from all time-series data like process parameters and energy parameters, a high-speed vibration data aggregation is also required.
  • Further, Phase 2 is supposed to be a Predictive analytics phase where proposed plan is to get weather data also for optimizing on energy needs and correct load assessments.
