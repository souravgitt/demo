HCL, being a renowned system integrator, has extensive experience of working with different platforms vendors and their related offering's (cloud as well as on premise). Based off that and the understanding HCL onsite team developed regarding Lilly's specific requirements and related operating context, HCL did an extensive comparative analysis from the available options in the market. The result of that analysis is shared in the subsequent sections. Although, Lilly expectation was to do comparison and suggest an optimum platform amongst PTC, AWS, Siemens Mindspheres and Azure, HCL also included one "open source" IoT platform (i.e. Eclipse) to the comparison mix. The reason being that in recent times, adoption of these "open source" platforms has increased significantly although these are still evolving from maturity perspective.

Although, HCL referred various analyst (e.g. Forrester) reports regarding platform comparison, however the intention has been to perform an unbiased evaluation and not to get influenced by these reports and other similar comparisons available on public domain. This is in-line with our approach of selecting the right fit platform which meets the stated and unstated needs as well as unique expectations of our customers while keeping in mind the future platform needs.

There are close to 450 small and large platforms available in the market today - selecting the right platform is no doubt a daunting task. To overcome this challenge, HCL has developed a quantitative approach for performing platform comparison which considers customer stated weightages for different parameters like cost, availability, deployment needs, future roadmap etc.to come up with a relative ranking of major platforms. Lilly team filled the weightages for parameters in the sheet attached in the references sections and based on that HCL has done platform evaluation and recommendation (said calculator is available in section #7.3Results of IIoT platform comparison.

HCL also understands that platform comparison would be incomplete in case only technical parameters are considered for evaluation purposes; in other words, factors like vendor system dynamics (e.g. PTC and Azure transitioning from competitors to partners) are equally important to be considered while arriving at the final recommendation. Similarly, market insights e.g. Azure consistently gaining market share compared to AWS were also considered.

In addition to leveraging in-house expertise and knowledge base for comparison and subsequent recommendation, HCL also held sessions with major platform vendors to understand their capabilities to meet Lilly specific requirements.
