HCL decision-making process regarding selection of a connectivity protocol follows the critical evaluation of all the connectivity offerings for IoT, through a well thought & live connectivity selection framework, as depicted above figure. The connectivity selection framework evaluates each connectivity protocol through three core stage gates of executive decision-making.

Technical Factors where focus is more on the robustness, maturity of technology and use case dependency where requirements can be further derived from parameters like minimum-range, power consumption, throughput/Data rate, security & scalability.

Deployment Factors where focus is more on manageability of network setup cost of deployment, managing spectrum (license & unlicensed band) & availability of vendor products.

Considering the above two factors we have used below parameters to evaluate the various short range (Wi-Fi, ZigBee, BLE Smart, HART and ISA100.11a) & long range (LoRaWAN, NBIoT and Private 5G) technologies.

  • Robustness
  • Range
  • Scalability Support
  • Security
  • Network Manageability
  • Power consumption (can device be Battery Powered)
  • Unlicensed Band
  • Mature Eco Syste

A funnel shaped decision tree has been developed after evaluating all these technologies leads to a conclusion that Lilly should choose from HART or ISA100.11a which is explained in the following table.

IoT Connectivity Decision Funnel

Connectivity Characteristics

Technology

Robustness/Interference Resistant: For Any kind of Industrial application, reliable message delivery, authentication & integrity are the key factors. Moreover, the threat of industrial espionage advances the requirement for encryption to hide information that can reveal anything about the production in the plant to competitors. ZigBee type of technologies have been lack of robustness. There is no diversity in the frequency and entire network share the static channel which could lead to easy signal jamming. This could also lead to fading because of metal rich environment. Single static channel allocation also increases the chances of interference.

BLE, Wi-Fi, ZigBee, Wireless HART & ISA100.11a, LoRaWAN, NBIoT, Private 5G

Range and Data rate: Data rate & range is also key consideration. The limitation with BLE & Wi-Fi is that, it has limited range of max 100 meter.

BLE, Wi-Fi, Wireless HART & ISA100.11a, LoRaWAN, NBIoT, Private 5G

Scalability Support: All long-range technologies have inherited Scalability Support; however, Wireless HART supports multiple access points per local area. For increased I/O data rates additional access points can be added Also for large plant more than one gateway can be used. ISA100.11a supports one or more backbone routers per local area network. If a local area exceeds the bandwidth of one radio, then additional backbone routers can be added.

Wireless HART & ISA100.11a, LoRaWAN, NBIoT, Private 5G

Security: LoRa, NBIoT, Private 5G, Wireless HART & ISA100.11a have a strong security mechanism. NBIoT uses LTE encryption, 5G has various flavor of security inherited from SNOW 3G, AES-CTR, ZUC, HMAC-SHA-256, 5G AKA, EAP, GDPR, ECIES, NESAS. Wireless HART uses Join Keys, Network Key & Session Keys. Session Keys are allocated for device to device communication. Join Keys are provisioned by hand handled device. LoRa & ISA100.11a also uses the same framework of keys (Join, session, device).

Wireless HART & ISA100.11a, LoRaWAN, NBIoT, Private 5G

Network Manageability: NBIoT services is provided by TSP like T-Mobile, AT&T & Sprint, Private 5G network can be provided by Telecom OEMs like Nokia & Ericson. LoRa can be provided by SENET or Comcast. Wireless HART & ISA100.11a is available in form of End-to-End service.

Wireless HART & ISA100.11a, LoRaWAN, NBIoT

Power Consumption: Except 5G, all other technologies support battery operated devices because of low power consumption.

Wireless HART & ISA100.11a, LoRaWAN, NBIoT

Unlicensed Spectrum: NBIoT uses license spectrum, however LoRA uses ISM band & Wireless HART & ISA100.11a use 2.4 GHz (unlicensed spectrum).

Wireless HART & ISA100.11a, LoRaWAN

Mature Eco-system: LoRaWAN is comparatively new, however Wireless HART & ISA100.11a are old, specifically designed for industrial application connectivity and backward compatible with old legacy systems too. Emerson, Siemens & ABB are leading players for Wireless HART. Honeywell & Yokogawa provides the support for ISA100.11a.
